ReadyAid

Check first

  • Check for any foreign objects embedded in the wound.
  • Assess if the bleeding is spurting or heavy.

Do this now

1

Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth.

2

Clean the area with water and mild soap once bleeding slows.

3

Apply an antibiotic ointment and a sterile bandage.

Do not

  • Do not remove any deeply embedded objects.
  • Do not use a tourniquet unless bleeding is life-threatening.

When to call emergency

  • If bleeding doesn't stop after 10 minutes of direct pressure.
  • If the wound is deep, gaping, or shows muscle/bone.

Note for you

Since you are on blood thinners, you must apply pressure for twice as long as usual.
